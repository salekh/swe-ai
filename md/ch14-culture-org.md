# Chapter 14: Software Engineering Culture & Organization in the AI Era

---

## The Cultural Shift

- From "I wrote it" to "I designed it"
- From "it compiles" to "it evaluates well"
- From "move fast and break things" to "move fast and measure everything"
- From individual heroics to team orchestration
- Embracing uncertainty: probabilistic systems require statistical evaluation

## Hiring and Skills Development

| Skill | Traditional Priority | AI-Era Priority |
|-------|---------------------|-----------------|
| Algorithmic coding | Very high | Moderate |
| System design | High | Very high |
| AI/ML literacy | Low | High |
| Evaluation design | Low | Very high |
| Curiosity & adaptability | Nice to have | Essential |

### AI Literacy Baseline (every engineer)
1. How LLMs work at a high level
2. Capabilities and limitations of current models
3. Prompt engineering and context management
4. Evaluation design (rubrics, golden datasets, LLM-as-Judge)
5. AI safety fundamentals
6. Cost structures of LLM inference

### Continuous Learning
- Monthly AI tech talks
- Quarterly hackathons
- AI reading groups
- Cross-team AI reviews

## Leadership in the AI Era

**Key decisions**: Investment allocation (30–40% infrastructure), risk calibration, team composition, vendor strategy (build vs. buy, API vs. self-hosted).

## Ethical Considerations

- Bias testing across demographic groups
- Transparency (users should know they're interacting with AI)
- Human override mechanisms
- Data privacy
- Environmental impact
