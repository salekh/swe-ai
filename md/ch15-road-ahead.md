# Chapter 15: The Road Ahead

---

## The Trajectory of Model Capabilities

### Reasoning and Planning
Next frontier: long-horizon planning (100+ steps), better architectural judgment, self-verification.

### Multimodality
Design-to-code from mockups, visual debugging from screenshots, video understanding for UX analysis.

### Efficiency
Today's frontier quality → tomorrow's commodity quality at 1/100th cost. Enables: per-request evaluation, longer agent chains, real-time AI.

## The Evolution of Software Engineering

### From Writing Code to Specifying Intent
Engineer's primary output shifts to: specifications, architecture, evaluation, review and validation.

### The Persistence of Fundamentals
More important than ever: distributed systems, security, performance engineering, data modeling.

### New Specializations
- Evaluation engineering
- Context engineering (evolved from prompt engineering)
- AI safety engineering
- Agent infrastructure engineering

## Industry Trends

- **Consolidation**: Converging on few providers, standardized protocols (MCP, A2A)
- **Regulation**: EU AI Act, transparency requirements, auditable reasoning traces, bias testing

## Career Advice

### What to Learn
1. System design (most durable)
2. AI literacy
3. Evaluation design
4. Communication
5. Domain expertise

### What Not to Worry About
- "AI will replace all programmers"
- "I need a PhD in ML"
- "I need to learn every new AI tool"

> **Key Takeaway**: The engineers who thrive will combine deep engineering fundamentals with AI fluency and strong communication skills. They will be architects, evaluators, and system designers. The code will increasingly be generated. The judgment, taste, and architectural vision will remain irreplaceably human.
